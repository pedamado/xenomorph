Who is Queen Rania?

Queen Rania al-Abdullah is the Queen Consort of the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan. Known globally for her intelligence, compassion, and modern leadership, she is a powerful voice for education, women's empowerment, and intercultural dialogue. She acts as a bridge between Arab traditions and the global community, embodying grace and progress in equal measure. Through her initiatives and humanitarian work, Queen Rania continues to inspire a generation that values knowledge, empathy, and unity across cultures.

Biography

Before Royalty

Born Rania Al-Yassin on August 31, 1970, in Kuwait City to Palestinian parents from Tulkarm in the West Bank, Queen Rania was raised in a family that deeply valued education and service. She attended the New English School in Kuwait and later earned a Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration from the American University in Cairo. After graduating, she began her career working at Citibank and later Apple Inc., experiences that broadened her perspective on international business and communication.

Skills

Queen Rania is known for her diplomacy, empathy, and forward-thinking leadership. Fluent in Arabic and English, she advocates for education reform, women’s empowerment, and youth innovation. She also uses digital platforms to promote dialogue, transparency, and understanding across cultures.

Notable Initiatives

Madrasati (My School)

Launched in 2008 to renovate underprivileged public schools in Jordan and improve learning environments.

Queen Rania Foundation for Education and Development (QRF)

Focuses on modernizing education, investing in early childhood learning, and supporting innovation in Arabic digital content.

Al-Aman Fund for the Future of Orphans

Established in 2003 to provide higher education and career support for orphaned youth in Jordan.

Edraak

A massive open online course (MOOC) platform offering free Arabic courses to learners across the Arab world.

1GOAL: Education for All

A global campaign advocating for universal access to primary education for children worldwide.

Timeline

  • 1970 — Born Rania Al-Yassin in Kuwait City to Palestinian parents.
  • 1991 — Graduates from the American University in Cairo with a degree in Business Administration.
  • 1993 — Marries Prince Abdullah bin Al-Hussein of Jordan.
  • 1999 — Becomes Queen Consort of Jordan after King Abdullah II’s ascension.
